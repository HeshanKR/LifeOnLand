Challenges

Since the industrial revolution the concentration of CO2 in the atmosphere has been increasing as a result of the burning fossil fuel. The marked increase in concentration of atmospheric CO2 over the last 150 years concerned scientists because it's linked to increased global temperature. Much of the solar radiation that's strike the planet is reflected back into space. Although CO2, methane, water vapor and other greenhouse gases in the atmosphere are transparent to visible light they intercept and absorb much of the infrared radiation. Earth emits re-reflecting some of it back to toward earth.
