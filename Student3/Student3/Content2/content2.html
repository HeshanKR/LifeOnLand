Tackling Deforestation: Protecting Our Planet’s Lungs

What is deforestation?

Deforestation is the purposeful clearing of forested land. Throughout history and into modern times, forests have been razed to make space for agriculture and animal grazing, and to obtain wood for fuel, manufacturing, and construction Throughout history and into modern times, forests have been razed to make space for agriculture and animal grazing, and to obtain wood for fuel, manufacturing, and construction.

Global Impact

Deforestation has significantly altered global landscapes, with 34% of Western Europe and 34% of North America's forests cut down for timber and agriculture. The largest deforestation is in tropical rainforests, aided by road construction and slash-and-burn agriculture. This leads to increased carbon dioxide emissions, threats to biodiversity, and soil erosion. While deforestation can be permanent, conservation efforts are allowing forests to return in some areas.

Distribution of forests

Forests, covering 30% of global land, provide essential resources like food, timber, energy, shelter, and medicine. However, they are threatened by deforestation, climate change, forest fires, and disease. While some forests have increased due to replanting, the loss of forests is expected to continue, necessitating effective forest management plans that balance environmental protection and human society's economic needs.

Burn deforestation

Amazon and Indonesian fires are primarily manmade due to illegal deforestation and farmland clearing. Wildfires are rare in tropical rainforests due to high humidity. Large-scale clearing disrupts rainforest's water absorption, storage, and recycling processes, making it fire-prone.

What is the solution to Deforestation?

Plant More Trees

Engage in tree-planting initiatives in your community or through global organizations.

Support Responsible Companies

Choose to support companies that are committed to reducing deforestation and use sustainable practices.

Go Paperless

Reduce paper consumption by going digital at home and in the office. When you do need to print, use both sides of the paper.

Buy and Use Responsibly

Consider your purchases carefully. Buy only what you need, and prioritize items with less packaging or reusable containers.

Support Sustainable Agriculture

Buy from local farmers who practice sustainable agriculture, helping to reduce the demand for deforested land.

Recycle and Buy Recycled Products

Support the recycling economy by buying recycled products and recycling them again after use.
